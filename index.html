How it works

Option A

Free doorstep pickup

  1. Tell us what you've got. A call, text, or email all work. Phones, laptops, PCs, tablets, DVRs, cables, whatever's piled up. Broken stuff is welcome, and honestly it's most of what we get.
  2. Pick a day. Choose a date that works for you, or just say "ASAP" and we'll slot you into this week's route.
  3. Set it out. Leave it on the porch or driveway, or hand it over in person. You'll get a text once it's collected.
Option B

Drop-off at the workshop

  1. Message us first. We'll send over the current drop-off address and hours.
  2. Bring your devices. Once you've got the details, there's no appointment needed.
  3. That's it. We take care of data wiping, refurbishing, and responsible recycling from there.

We accept: phones · laptops · desktop PCs · tablets · flat-screen monitors · DVRs & streaming boxes · game consoles · routers & modems · keyboards, mice & cables · hard drives (wiped or destroyed), plus just about any other electronic. If it plugs in or charges, chances are we'll take it. Not sure? Ask us.

Why it matters

One device can change a kid's whole year

Thousands of Bay Area students still don't have a working computer at home. Homework gets done on a cracked phone screen, or it doesn't get done at all. Meanwhile, perfectly fixable laptops sit forgotten in drawers a few blocks over. Safe Cycle Tech exists to close that gap.

What we do with your device

Every donation gets sorted by hand at the workshop. If a device can be saved, we wipe it, repair it, load it with free kid-friendly software, and pass it along. If it can't, certified California recyclers recover its materials, so nothing toxic ends up in our soil or water.

Where devices go

Refurbished computers, phones, tablets, and anything else that can help a student get donated to families through local channels that serve low-income youth around the Bay Area:

  • Title I schools and after-school programs
  • Community centers serving low-income families
  • Youth and family programs across the Bay Area

Our story

Helping Bay Area kids get connected

Safe Cycle Tech is a small Bay Area workshop with a single goal: getting students and underprivileged families online. We collect the electronics you're done with, refurbish what we can, and get working devices to local kids who need them. Whatever can't be saved is recycled responsibly.

The need is bigger than most people realize. Plenty of students right here in the Bay Area still don't have a working computer at home, and these days homework, school portals, college applications, and job hunts all happen online. A family without a device falls behind. Your old electronics can change that.

Straight answers

Questions people actually ask

Is it really free? What's the catch?

Really free, no catch. Refurbishing donated electronics for local kids is the whole point of the workshop. Down the road we may pursue nonprofit status and California's recycling programs to help fund growth, but pickups stay free regardless.

What happens to my personal data?

Every storage drive is wiped to NIST 800-88 standards or physically destroyed before a device leaves our hands. If you can, backing up and clearing your personal files first is a smart extra precaution.

My device is completely dead. Do you still want it?

Yes. Even dead devices hold recoverable parts, along with hazardous materials that shouldn't go anywhere near a landfill. If we can't revive it, it heads to a certified California electronics recycler.

Who receives the devices?

Refurbished computers, phones, and tablets go to underprivileged students through schools, after-school programs, and community organizations that serve low-income Bay Area families.

Do you take TVs, printers, or big appliances?

Computer monitors, yes, as long as they're the flat-screen kind. What we can't take: old tube-style (CRT) TVs and monitors, big-screen TVs, and large appliances. We're a small operation, so oversized, heavy items are beyond what we can haul. For pretty much everything else the rule of thumb is simple: if it plugs in or charges and one person can carry it, we'll almost certainly take it. Not sure? Ask us, and if it's something we can't handle we'll point you to the right county drop-off.
